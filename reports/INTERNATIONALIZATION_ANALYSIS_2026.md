# 1. WHERE TO CREATE THE COMPANY

## Executive Summary

**Recommendation: Spain (Sociedad Limitada - SL)** under the Ley de Startups framework, with potential restructuring to Ireland or Netherlands only once revenue exceeds EUR 500K+ and IP licensing becomes meaningful.

The founder lives in Valencia, Spain. Any foreign entity (Estonia, Delaware, Dubai) managed from Spain will trigger permanent establishment rules, meaning Spain will tax the profits anyway. The only legitimate path to foreign incorporation requires real economic substance abroad -- employees, offices, and genuine management decisions outside Spain. For an early-stage SaaS, this is impractical and financially wasteful.

---

## Detailed Jurisdiction Comparison

### SPAIN (Sociedad Limitada - SL) -- RECOMMENDED

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Corporate Tax** | 15% for qualifying startups (first 4 years under Ley de Startups); 25% standard rate; 21% for companies under EUR 1M revenue (2025); drops to 19% on first EUR 50K + 21% remainder (2026) |
| **VAT** | 21% standard rate |
| **Formation Cost** | EUR 1,000-3,000 (notary, registry, legal fees) |
| **Minimum Capital** | EUR 1 (under Ley Crea y Crece; EUR 3,000 recommended) |
| **Timeline** | 6 hours (express with standard bylaws) to 3-4 weeks (standard) |
| **Stripe** | Fully supported; Spain is a Stripe-supported country |
| **Banking** | Easy; local banks in Valencia (CaixaBank, BBVA, Sabadell, Santander) |
| **IP Protection** | EU trademark and patent systems available; OEPM for national IP |

**Pros:**
- No permanent establishment risk (founder lives there)
- Ley de Startups benefits: 15% corporate tax for 4 years, deferred tax payments, reduced notary/registry fees, stock option advantages
- Full EU market access, EU trademark protection
- Simplest compliance -- one jurisdiction, one tax authority
- Spain-EU double tax treaties with 90+ countries
- Stripe fully supported with EUR payments
- Digital invoicing mandate from January 2026 (you need this for SaaS anyway)
- Access to EU OSS (One-Stop-Shop) for cross-border VAT

**Cons:**
- Higher social security costs (autonomo contributions ~EUR 300-500/month as administrator)
- 25% standard corporate tax rate (after startup period)
- Bureaucratic processes relative to Estonia or UK
- Wealth tax exists (though Valencia doubled exemption to EUR 1M in 2025)

### ESTONIA (e-Residency / OU)

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Corporate Tax** | 0% on retained profits; 22% on distributed profits (2026) |
| **VAT** | 24% (increased July 2025) |
| **Formation Cost** | EUR 100 (e-Residency card) + EUR 265 (state fee) + EUR 500-1,500 (service provider) |
| **Minimum Capital** | EUR 0.01 |
| **Timeline** | 2-4 weeks for e-Residency card; 1-2 days for company registration |
| **Stripe** | Supported (Estonia is a Stripe-supported country) |
| **Banking** | Difficult for non-residents; many banks reject; alternatives like Wise Business, Payoneer |

**Pros:**
- 0% tax on reinvested profits (excellent for growth-stage)
- 100% online administration
- Low formation costs
- EU market access

**Cons:**
- CRITICAL: Spanish resident managing an Estonian company = permanent establishment in Spain. Spain will tax profits attributable to Spanish operations. The Spanish Tax Agency (AEAT) actively investigates these structures
- Banking is problematic; traditional banks frequently reject e-Residency companies
- Need local contact person and address in Estonia (EUR 500-1,000/year)
- Mandatory accounting services (EUR 100-300/month)
- No real tax benefit if managed from Spain -- you pay Spanish tax anyway
- 2026 tightening of "economic substance" rules across the EU
- Higher VAT (24%) than Spain (21%)

**Verdict:** Not recommended. The 0% tax advantage evaporates when the founder lives in Spain. The AEAT considers the company Spanish tax resident if effective management is in Spain, and the burden of proof is on the taxpayer.

# 2. WHEN TO CREATE THE COMPANY

## Revenue/User Thresholds for Incorporation

### Immediate (Before First Sale)

| Trigger | Why |
|---------|-----|
| **Accepting payments from customers** | Operating commercially without a registered entity means personal unlimited liability. Stripe requires a business entity for full functionality |
| **GDPR compliance** | As a data processor/controller of EU personal data, you need a legal entity as the "controller" identified in your privacy policy |
| **Terms of Service** | Customers need to contract with a legal entity, not a natural person |
| **Professional credibility** | Vacation rental hosts (your B2B customers) expect to contract with a company |

### The Tax Optimization Point

| Income Level | Recommended Structure |
|-------------|----------------------|
| **< EUR 15K/year** | Autonomo (self-employed) could work initially, but SL is still recommended for liability |
| **EUR 15K - 60K/year** | SL becomes clearly advantageous: 15% corporate tax (startup) vs. up to 37% IRPF marginal rate as autonomo |
| **> EUR 60K/year** | SL is unambiguously better. Autonomo IRPF rates reach 45%+ vs. 15-25% corporate tax |

### Stripe Requirements

- Stripe allows sole proprietors (autonomos) to accept payments in Spain
- However, a registered business entity (SL) provides:
  - Higher transaction limits
  - Business verification (blue checkmark equivalent)
  - Access to Stripe Billing, Invoicing, and Tax products
  - Multi-currency payouts
  - Custom branding on checkout
- Stripe Atlas can form a US entity (Delaware C-Corp or LLC) for USD 500 if you want a US presence

### GDPR Compliance Timeline

GDPR compliance is required from day one if you:
- Process personal data of EU residents (you do -- host names, emails, property data)
- Use cookies or tracking (you likely do)
- Store data in any cloud service

**Minimum GDPR requirements before launch:**
1. Privacy Policy identifying the data controller (legal entity)
2. Cookie consent mechanism
3. Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) with all subprocessors (Supabase, Vercel, Stripe, Resend, etc.)
4. Lawful basis for each type of data processing
5. Data subject rights mechanism (access, deletion, portability)
6. Records of processing activities (ROPA)

### Legal Necessity Points

| Event | Action Required |
|-------|----------------|
| First paying customer | Must have entity + invoicing capability |
| Processing personal data | GDPR controller identification |
| Hiring employees/contractors | Employment law compliance |
| Revenue > EUR 60K | Financial optimization strongly favors SL |
| EU cross-border sales > EUR 10K | EU OSS VAT registration |
| January 2026 | Electronic invoicing mandatory for all SLs in Spain |

## RECOMMENDATION: Incorporate NOW

Given that Itineramio already has users and processes personal data, the company should be formed as a Spanish SL immediately. The Ley de Startups benefits (15% tax, deferred payments, reduced fees) make this the optimal window. Delaying creates:
- Personal liability exposure
- GDPR compliance gaps
- Inability to issue proper invoices
- Risk of penalties from the AEAT

# 3. STEPS TO CREATE THE COMPANY (Spain SL under Ley de Startups)

## Pre-Formation Checklist

- [ ] Spanish NIE (Numero de Identificacion de Extranjero) -- already have this if resident in Valencia
- [ ] DNI/NIE of all founders
- [ ] Digital certificate (Certificado Digital) from the FNMT
- [ ] Decide on company name (prepare 3-5 alternatives)
- [ ] Define share structure and initial capital
- [ ] Prepare business plan (needed for Ley de Startups qualification)

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Obtain Digital Certificate (if not already held)
- **What:** Certificado Digital from the FNMT (Fabrica Nacional de Moneda y Timbre)
- **How:** Request online at sede.fnmt.gob.es, then verify identity at a registration office
- **Cost:** Free
- **Timeline:** 1-3 days

### Step 2: Reserve Company Name
- **What:** Certificado Negativo de Denominacion Social from the Registro Mercantil Central
- **How:** Online at rmc.es or through a PAE (Punto de Atencion al Emprendedor)
- **Cost:** EUR 15-20 (online) or EUR 20-40 (in person)
- **Timeline:** 1-3 business days (online); same day at PAE
- **Note:** Reserve 3 names in order of preference; certificate valid for 6 months

### Step 3: Open Provisional Bank Account and Deposit Capital
- **What:** Open a business bank account and deposit minimum share capital
- **How:** Visit a bank in Valencia with the name reservation certificate
- **Recommended banks:** CaixaBank (startup-friendly), BBVA, Sabadell
- **Capital:** Minimum EUR 1 (under Ley Crea y Crece), recommended EUR 3,000
- **Cost:** EUR 0-50/month (depending on bank and plan)
- **Timeline:** 1-5 business days
- **Output:** Bank certificate of capital deposit

### Step 4: Draft Bylaws (Estatutos Sociales)
- **What:** Company bylaws defining governance, share structure, administrator powers
- **How:** Use standard bylaws (model templates available at CIRCE) or customize with a lawyer
- **Cost:** EUR 0 (standard) or EUR 300-800 (custom with lawyer)
- **Timeline:** 1-3 days

### Step 5: Sign Public Deed at Notary (Escritura Publica)
- **What:** The notary formalizes the incorporation deed with all founders present
- **Required documents:**
  - Name reservation certificate
  - Bank deposit certificate
  - DNI/NIE of all founders
  - Bylaws
  - Founder identification
- **Cost:** EUR 150-500 (notary fees; reduced under Ley de Startups for standard bylaws)
- **Timeline:** 1-2 hours appointment; deed ready in 1-3 days
- **Note:** Under Ley de Startups with standard bylaws, notary fees are significantly reduced

### Step 6: Obtain CIF (Tax Identification Number)
- **What:** Provisional CIF from the Agencia Tributaria (AEAT)
- **How:** File Form 036 (Declaracion censal) at the AEAT, either online or in person
- **Cost:** Free
- **Timeline:** Immediate (provisional CIF)
- **Note:** Provisional CIF allows you to start operations; becomes definitive after registry

### Step 7: Register at the Commercial Registry (Registro Mercantil)
- **What:** Register the public deed at the Provincial Commercial Registry of Valencia
- **How:** Submit the notarized deed
- **Cost:** EUR 100-300
- **Timeline:** 5-15 business days (standard); faster under startup express procedures
- **Output:** Company officially exists with legal personality

### Step 8: Apply for Startup Status (ENISA / Ley de Startups)
- **What:** Apply for recognition as an "empresa emergente" (emerging company) to access Ley de Startups benefits
- **How:** Through the ENISA platform (enisa.es)
- **Requirements:**
  - Company less than 5 years old (7 for biotech/energy/industrial)
  - Headquarters or majority of workforce in Spain
  - At least 60% of employees on Spanish contracts
  - Annual revenue under EUR 10M
  - Not resulting from a merger of non-innovative companies
  - Innovative project with scalable business model
- **Cost:** Free
- **Timeline:** Variable (weeks to months for approval)
- **Benefit:** Once approved, 15% corporate tax for 4 years, deferred tax payments, reduced fees

### Step 9: Register for Social Security (Administrador)
- **What:** Register the administrator (you) with the RETA (Regimen Especial de Trabajadores Autonomos)
- **How:** Through the Tesoreria General de la Seguridad Social
- **Cost:** EUR 80/month (tarifa plana for new autonomos, first 12 months) rising to ~EUR 300-500/month
- **Timeline:** Must be done within 30 days of registration

### Step 10: Set Up Stripe Business Account
- **What:** Upgrade to or create a Stripe business account linked to the SL
- **How:** At dashboard.stripe.com:
  1. Create account or convert existing personal account
  2. Provide CIF number
  3. Provide company name and registered address
  4. Link the business bank account (IBAN)
  5. Provide administrator identification (DNI/NIE)
  6. Complete identity verification
- **Cost:** Stripe fees only (1.5% + EUR 0.25 for European cards; 2.5% + EUR 0.25 for non-European)
- **Timeline:** 1-3 business days for verification
- **Note:** You can start accepting payments immediately with some limits during verification

### Step 11: GDPR Compliance Setup
- **What:** Prepare all required legal documents and technical measures
- **Actions:**
  - Draft Privacy Policy (identify SL as data controller)
  - Implement cookie consent (CookieBot, CookieScript, or similar)
  - Execute DPAs with subprocessors (Supabase, Vercel, Stripe, Resend, etc.)
  - Create Records of Processing Activities (ROPA)
  - Implement data subject rights mechanism
  - Register with the AEPD (Spanish Data Protection Agency) if required
- **Cost:** EUR 200-1,000 (DIY with templates) or EUR 1,500-5,000 (with legal firm)
- **Timeline:** 1-2 weeks

# 4. REGULATIONS BY TARGET MARKET

## PRIORITY MARKETS

---

### SPAIN (Home Market)

**Data Protection:**
- GDPR (directly applicable) + LOPDGDD (Ley Organica de Proteccion de Datos y Garantia de Derechos Digitales)
- Supervisory authority: AEPD (Agencia Espanola de Proteccion de Datos)
- Fines: Up to EUR 20M or 4% of global turnover

**Consumer Protection (SaaS):**
- Ley General para la Defensa de los Consumidores y Usuarios
- 14-day withdrawal right for online purchases (B2C)
- Clear pricing, no hidden fees
- Spanish-language Terms of Service required for B2C
- EU Data Act (September 2025): Customers can terminate with 2-month notice; data portability mandatory; switching fees prohibited from January 2027

**VAT:**
- 21% standard rate on digital services
- Must charge VAT from the first sale
- Quarterly VAT returns (Modelo 303)
- Annual summary (Modelo 390)
- SII (Suministro Inmediato de Informacion) for large companies
- EU OSS available for cross-border B2C sales to other EU countries

**Tourism-Specific:**
- Your platform helps hosts create digital guides -- you are NOT a short-term rental platform (like Airbnb/Booking), so Regulation (EU) 2024/1028 on STR data-sharing does NOT directly apply to you
- However, you should be aware that hosts must comply with VUD (Ventana Unica Digital) registration from July 2025
- No specific licensing required for a content creation SaaS
- If you include booking links or facilitate reservations, you could be classified as an intermediary

**Cookie Consent:**
- LSSI (Ley de Servicios de la Sociedad de la Informacion): Prior informed consent required for non-essential cookies
- Must provide granular cookie choices (accept, reject, configure)
- Cookie policy must be accessible from every page

**Invoicing:**
- From January 2026: Electronic invoicing mandatory for all SLs (Ley Crea y Crece)
- Must use certified invoicing software

### UNITED KINGDOM

**Data Protection:**
- UK GDPR + Data Protection Act 2018 + Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 (DUAA)
- Supervisory authority: ICO (Information Commissioner's Office)
- Fines: Up to GBP 17.5M or 4% of global turnover
- EU-UK adequacy decision extended; data flows continue freely
- DUAA 2025 changes: Simplified legitimate interests, streamlined DSARs, relaxed low-risk cookie consent

**Consumer Protection (SaaS):**
- Consumer Rights Act 2015
- Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013
- 14-day cooling-off period for online B2C purchases
- Digital content must be of satisfactory quality, fit for purpose, and as described
- Automatic renewal terms must be transparent

**VAT:**
- 20% standard rate
- Non-UK businesses: No registration threshold -- must register from the first sale to UK consumers
- UK businesses: GBP 90,000 threshold
- Making Tax Digital: Compatible software required for VAT; Corporation Tax from 2026

**Tourism-Specific:**
- No specific regulations affecting a SaaS content creation platform
- UK short-term rental regulations vary by region (England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland)
- London: 90-day limit on short-term lets without planning permission
- Scotland: Licensing scheme for short-term lets
- Your platform is not affected directly but hosts must comply

**Cookie Consent:**
- PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations)
- DUAA 2025: Low-risk cookies (analytics, security) may be used without consent if opt-out is available
- Marketing and tracking cookies still require explicit consent

**Payment Methods:**
- Credit/debit cards (dominant)
- Apple Pay, Google Pay
- Open Banking (growing)

### FRANCE

**Consumer Protection (SaaS):**

**VAT:**

**Payment Methods:**

- SEPA Direct Debit
- Apple Pay, Google Pay

## EU Data Act Compliance (Mandatory from September 2025)

Since Itineramio is an EU-based SaaS platform, the EU Data Act creates specific obligations:

1. **Switching Rights:** Customers can terminate any contract with maximum 2-month notice
2. **Data Portability:** Must allow customers to export all their data in machine-readable format
3. **No Lock-In:** Cannot impose contractual, technical, or organizational barriers to switching
4. **Switching Charges:** Reduced charges permitted until January 2027; fully prohibited after
5. **Transparency:** Must publish documentation on data formats, interoperability, and migration procedures

**Action Items:**
